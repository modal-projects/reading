## Overview

The narrow question here is not "how do MoE models work?" The job is to build a repeatable
training motion for customers near Modal's golden path, not to support every exotic workload.
Right now, large MoE support is the blocker for current customer work, with the model focus
moving from GLM to Kimi. That means the practical question is not "how did DeepSeek train V3
from scratch?" It is "which pieces of the latest OSS MoE stack are required if Modal wants
large-model LoRA, SFT, and RL runs to feel boring?"

The newest open reports converge on a familiar set of answers. Frontier MoE models keep active
parameters modest while letting total parameters grow. They lean heavily on stable routing and
load balancing, aggressive precision choices, specialized expert kernels, and expert-parallel
communication that is treated as a first-class systems problem. They also mix long pretraining
with serious post-training, including reinforcement learning and agentic data generation.

For Modal, not all of that should be copied literally. DeepSeek-V3 and Kimi K2 are useful
because they show the outer envelope of current OSS MoE training. But the current wedge is
closer to post-training existing OSS checkpoints on clusters around the 64-GPU shape, using
Miles-friendly workflows and infrastructure that makes multi-node runs predictable. This spike
focuses on the techniques that survive that translation.

## Context

- **Large MoE support is the blocker** for current customer work.
- The goal is **repeatable golden-path training**, not a one-off trillion-token pretraining
  effort.
- The current framework bet is **Miles**, with explicit pressure to support **GLM/Kimi-class
  models** and migrate model paths toward more Megatron-compatible tooling.
- The platform bottlenecks are well known: **predictable multi-node capacity, RDMA, and
  compute-colocated storage**.

## Key themes

- The frontier MoE reports all buy efficiency by keeping **active parameters modest** while total parameters scale up.
- The techniques most likely to matter on Modal are **stable routing, FP8, grouped or dropless expert kernels, and fast expert-parallel communication**.
- For the current goals, **post-training existing checkpoints** is much more aligned than reproducing 15T to 23T-token pretraining runs.
- Modal's differentiator is not absolute cluster scale; it is making **64-GPU-ish MoE runs predictable, restartable, and storage-local**.
- A credible golden path likely needs **Miles-level ergonomics** on top of **Megatron and DeepEP-class kernels and comms**.

## How we could train these efficiently on Modal

- Treat DeepSeek, Kimi, and GLM as recipe inputs, not as a mandate to recreate full frontier pretraining. The near-term target should be **LoRA, SFT, and RL on existing open checkpoints**.
- Keep **Miles** as the user-facing training loop, but make sure the backend path can expose **expert parallelism, grouped GEMM, FP8, and DeepEP-style dispatch** cleanly. The real question is not Miles or Megatron; it is whether Miles can drive a serious MoE backend.
- Keep cluster shapes boring: fixed-size multi-node jobs, predictable gang scheduling or reservations, and region-local data. MoE training is more sensitive to scheduling jitter than ordinary dense fine-tuning.
- Invest in **RDMA and colocated storage together**. Faster expert dispatch helps, but checkpointing and dataset reads still dominate if data and weights ride the public internet.
- Default to stability-first choices from the current reports: **bias-based or auxiliary-loss-free balancing, careful optimizer selection, FP8 where supported, and frequent async checkpoints**.
- Defer the wrong problems. Fully elastic clusters, mixed GPU types, and from-scratch 1T-scale pretraining are not the right first milestone for the current wedge.

## Suggested reading order

1. Start with **DeepSeek-V3** for the clearest public example of frontier-scale open MoE training decisions.
2. Read **Kimi K2** next because it is the closest public analogue to the model family being actively chased.
3. Read **GLM-4.5** for a stronger view of the multi-stage post-training stack, especially expert model iteration and RL.
4. Read **MegaBlocks** to understand why MoE training becomes a kernel problem as soon as routing gets dynamic.
5. Read the **Megatron Core MoE** docs as an implementation checklist for the current production stack.
6. Finish with **DeepEP** for the communication layer that most directly maps onto Modal's RDMA and multi-node work.
