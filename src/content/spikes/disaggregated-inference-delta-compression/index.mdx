## Overview

This spike is narrower than a generic survey of disaggregated serving. The concrete target is
**autoscaling, disaggregated inference for RL** on Modal, with SGLang coordination as the main
learning vehicle in the Perplexity/Tinker direction. Rollout and training separation is a good
fit for Modal's capacity model if weight movement can be made cheap.

Disaggregated serving first became popular because prefill and decode want different resources.
RL post-training pushed the same architectural instinct further: keep rollout and training
separate so each cluster can specialize. The catch is that specialization creates a
synchronization boundary. If updated weights cannot move across that boundary cheaply, the
architecture becomes unstable or too slow to matter.

That is why delta compression matters here. It is not an abstract optimization; it is the
difference between a nice architecture diagram and a practical Modal example. The most useful
reading on this topic comes from systems teams who had to make disaggregated training and
inference work in production.

## Modal context

- The operating model fits **Modal's capacity model** and is worth chasing directly.
- A good artifact to publish is an example of **delta-compressed weight updates** in the
  multinode training guide.
- The Perplexity/Tinker item is a learning vehicle for **SGLang coordination**.
- Disaggregating the training cluster from the rollout pool and using better synchronization
  and orchestration is what keeps the split economical.

## Key themes

- Disaggregation improves utilization only if **state transfer** stops being the dominant cost.
- The concrete target is not generic serving throughput but **RL rollout/training separation on Modal**.
- Delta compression is attractive because RL updates are often structured and sparse relative to full weight snapshots.
- The winning systems pair compression with **point-to-point transfer**, pipelining, and static scheduling.
- The real operational question is not just speed, but whether weight sync can stay fast enough to avoid train-infer drift while keeping rollout pools warm and autoscalable.
- Cross-datacenter PD is becoming plausible once KVCache shrinks (hybrid attention), but only with **selective offloading and bandwidth-aware scheduling** to survive bursty, skewed workloads.

## Suggested reading order

1. Start with **Disaggregated Inference: 18 Months Later** for the architectural frame.
2. Read **Disaggregated Prefill and Decode** to understand the concrete serving split before adding RL-specific weight sync.
3. Read **Weight Transfer for RL Post-Training in under 2 seconds** as the closest public analogue to the Perplexity/Tinker workstream.
4. Read **RDMA Point-to-Point Communication for LLM Systems** for the transport layer ideas that make the previous step reusable.
5. Read **Prefill-as-a-Service** to see how far PD disaggregation stretches once KVCache shrinks and prefill can ride commodity Ethernet across clusters.
6. Finish with **PULSE** to evaluate whether sparse delta-style synchronization can be the next optimization once the basic disaggregated path is working.
